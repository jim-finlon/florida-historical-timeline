# Dr. Samuel Mudd: The Prisoner of Fort Jefferson
## 1865-1869

---

## Overview

When Dr. Samuel Mudd set the broken leg of a stranger who knocked on his door in the early morning hours of April 15, 1865, he sealed his fate as one of history's most controversial figures. The stranger was John Wilkes Booth, fleeing after assassinating President Abraham Lincoln. Mudd was convicted of conspiracy in the assassination, sentenced to life imprisonment, and sent to Fort Jeffersonâ€”a massive brick fortress on a remote island in the Dry Tortugas, 70 miles west of Key West. There, in conditions of brutal isolation, Mudd served nearly four years before a presidential pardon freed him. Was he a conspirator or a victim of circumstance? The debate continues, but his imprisonment at Fort Jefferson remains one of the strangest chapters in both Civil War and Florida history.

- Needed medical attention
- Knew Dr. Mudd
- Arrived at his farm

- Country physician

**The Treatment:**
- April 15, 1865, pre-dawn
- Set Booth's broken leg

## The Question

### Did Mudd Know?

**The Prosecution Case:**
- Mudd knew Booth from previous meetings
- Confederate sympathizer
- Part of conspiracy
- Guilty of treason

**The Defense:**
- Doctor's duty to treat
- Didn't recognize disguised Booth
- Innocent victim
- Caught in circumstances

### The Evidence

**Against Mudd:**
- Prior meetings with Booth documented
- Lied to investigators initially
- Slow to report suspicious patient
- Known Confederate sympathies

**For Mudd:**
- Early morning, stranger in disguise
- Doctor's obligation

## The Trial

### The Military Commission

**The Process:**
- Military tribunal, not civilian court
- Emotionally charged atmosphere
- Lincoln just assassinated
- Nation wanted justice

**The Verdict:**
- Found guilty of conspiracy
- Sentenced to life imprisonment
- Escaped death penalty by one vote
- August 1865

### The Sentence

**Fort Jefferson:**
- Remote island prison
- Dry Tortugas, Florida
- 70 miles from Key West
- Harshest federal prison

## Fort Jefferson

### The Fortress

**The Structure:**
- Massive hexagonal fort
- 16 million bricks
- Built 1846-1875 (never completed)
- Largest masonry structure in Americas

**The Location:**
- Garden Key, Dry Tortugas
- 70 miles west of Key West
- Utterly remote
- No escape possible

### The Prison

**The Conditions:**
- Military prison during/after Civil War
- Harsh discipline
- Tropical heat
- Disease rampant

**The Inmates:**
- Military prisoners
- Lincoln conspirators
- Various criminals
- Brutal conditions
