# Cassadaga: The Psychic Capital of the World
## 1894-Present

---

## Overview

In 1894, George P. Colby, a spiritualist medium from New York, founded a community of like-minded believers in the Florida scrub near DeLand, following what he said was the guidance of his Native American spirit guide named "Seneca." The community he established, Cassadaga, has survived for over 130 years as America's oldest active spiritualist community. Today, this tiny village of Victorian cottages and weathered hotels remains home to certified mediums and healers who offer readings, s√©ances, and spiritual guidance to the thousands of visitors who arrive each year seeking contact with the dead, insight into the future, or simply a glimpse of the unusual. Cassadaga is a living museum of American spiritualism, unchanged in an unchanging landscape.

## The Founder

### George P. Colby

**The Background:**
- Born 1848, Pike, New York
- Tuberculosis sufferer
- Discovered mediumship as young man
- Associated with Lily Dale, New York (spiritualist community)

**The Vision:**
- Spirit guide "Seneca" (said to be a Native American)
- Told to go south for health
- Guided to specific location
- Would found a community

**The Journey:**
- Came to Florida 1875
- Found the land
- Returned north to gather followers
- Established community 1894

### The Spiritualist Movement

**The Origins:**
- Fox Sisters, 1848, Hydesville, New York
- "Spirit rappings"
- Communication with dead claimed
- Movement swept America

**The Beliefs:**
- Spirits of dead survive
- Can communicate with living
- Mediums facilitate contact
- Progressive social views often

## The Community

### The Founding

**1894:**
- Southern Cassadaga Spiritualist Camp Meeting Association
- Named after Cassadaga, New York
- 35 acres initially
- Spiritualist community established

**The Structure:**
- Non-profit religious organization
- Land held in common
- Members lease property
- Self-governing

### The Early Years

**The Buildings:**
- Simple cottages built
- Cassadaga Hotel (1901)
- Auditorium for services
- Camp meeting style

**The Life:**
- Seasonal at first (winter months)
- Mediums offered services
- Spiritual teachings
- Community rituals

### The Growth

**The Expansion:**
- Year-round community developed
- More buildings added
- Recognition grew
- Visitors came

**The Character:**
- Victorian architecture
- Quiet streets
- Oak trees with Spanish moss
- Time seems stopped
