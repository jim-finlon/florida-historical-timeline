# Spring Break: Fort Lauderdale and the College Invasion
## 1936-Present

---

## Overview

Spring Break as an American institution began in Fort Lauderdale, Florida, in 1936 when a college swim coach brought his team south to train in the warm weather. By the 1950s, word had spread, and college students began descending on Fort Lauderdale every March and April. The phenomenon exploded after the 1960 film *Where the Boys Are*, which portrayed Fort Lauderdale as a paradise of sun, surf, and romance. For the next three decades, Fort Lauderdale was the epicenter of Spring Break, hosting hundreds of thousands of students annually. But by the late 1980s, fed up with the chaos, the city cracked down, and Spring Break migrated to Panama City Beach, Daytona Beach, South Beach, and beyond. ## The Origins

### The First Spring Breakers

**1936:**
- Colgate University swimming coach brought team to Fort Lauderdale
- Trained at the Casino Pool (a municipal pool)
- Warm weather, outdoor swimming in winter
- Word spread among athletic programs

**The 1930s-1940s:**
- More college swim teams came
- Athletic training in sun
- Not yet a mass phenomenon
- But foundation laid

### The College Town

**Fort Lauderdale's Appeal:**
- Beautiful beach
- Warm March weather
- Affordable accommodations
- Welcoming atmosphere (initially)

**The Growth:**
- Swimmers told friends
- Fraternities and sororities organized trips
- Non-athletes discovered Florida
- By 1950s, a tradition

## *Where the Boys Are* (1960)

### The Movie

**The Film:**
- Released December 1960
- Based on Glendon Swarthout novel
- Set during Spring Break in Fort Lauderdale
- Connie Francis title song

**The Plot:**
- Four college girls go to Fort Lauderdale
- Looking for fun and romance
- Mix of comedy and drama
- Beach, bars, boys

**The Impact:**
- Massive hit
- Made Fort Lauderdale synonymous with Spring Break
- National advertising for the tradition
- Changed everything

### The Explosion

**After the Movie:**
- Students flooded Fort Lauderdale
- Numbers skyrocketed
- National media covered it
- Became cultural phenomenon

**The Numbers:**

| Year | Estimated Spring Breakers |
|------|--------------------------|
| 1960 | 20,000 |
| 1965 | 50,000 |
| 1970 | 100,000 |
| 1985 | 350,000+ |

## The Fort Lauderdale Era

### The Scene

**The Strip:**
- A1A along the beach
- Hotels, bars, pools
- Students everywhere
- March madness

**The Beach:**
- Wall-to-wall bodies
- Volleyball, frisbee
- Sunbathing, swimming
- Social scene

**The Elbo Room:**
- Bar at the center of action
- Featured in *Where the Boys Are*
- Famous Spring Break destination
- Still exists

### The Culture

**The Rituals:**
- Arrive by car or bus
- Pack into cheap hotels
- Hit the beach days
- Hit the bars nights
- Repeat

**The Behavior:**
- Drinking (legal age was 18 until 1987)
- Dancing
- Flirting
- Sometimes chaos

**The Freedom:**
- Away from parents
- Away from campus
- Brief liberation
- Coming-of-age ritual

### The Industry

**The Money:**
- Hotels booked solid
- Bars and restaurants packed
- Shops sold souvenirs
- Hundreds of millions in spending

**The Tension:**
- Revenue vs. rowdiness
- Residents complained
- Businesses profited
- Balance always difficult

---

## The Problems

### The Costs

**For the City:**
- Police overtime
- Trash and cleanup
- Property damage
- Complaints from residents

**The Behavior:**
- Public drunkenness
- Fights
- Sexual assault (often unreported)
- Occasional deaths

**The Crowds:**
- Overwhelmed infrastructure
- Traffic gridlock
- Beach overcrowding
- Local life disrupted

### The Crackdown

**Late 1980s:**
- Fort Lauderdale had enough
- New ordinances: open container laws, noise limits
- Police enforcement increased
- Message: "You're not welcome"

**1987:**
- Drinking age raised to 21 nationally
- Core appeal diminished
- Fewer legal drinkers
- Shift began

**The Result:**
- Spring Breakers looked elsewhere
- Fort Lauderdale successfully ended the tradition
- But lost the revenue
- Tourism rebuilt on different model

## Spring Break Migrates

### Panama City Beach

**The New Capital:**
- Northwestern Florida panhandle
- Took Fort Lauderdale's place
- 1980s-2010s: major destination
- "Spring Break Capital of the World"

**The Scene:**
- Beaches, bars, clubs
- Cheap hotels
- MTV broadcasts
- Same chaos, new location

**The Crackdown:**
- By 2015, Panama City also had enough
- New ordinances
- Reduced dramatically
- Pattern repeated

### Other Destinations

**Daytona Beach:**
- Another major destination
- More motorcycle week, but also Spring Break
- Beaches permitted cars (unique)
- Eventually reduced

**South Beach, Miami:**
- Upscale alternative
- Different vibe
- Hip-hop Spring Break
- Urban beach scene

**Key West:**
- Smaller scale
- More alternative crowd
- LGBTQ Spring Break
- Different tradition

**Cancun, Jamaica, Bahamas:**
- International alternatives
- No age 21 limit abroad
- Package deals
- Florida's loss, Caribbean's gain

## The Phenomenon

### What It Meant

**As Ritual:**
- Marking of college life
- Between winter and final exams
- Coming-of-age moment
- Collective memory

**As Industry:**
- Billions of dollars
- Employment for locals
- Hotel bookings
- Economic engine

**As Problem:**
- Public health (alcohol, drugs)
- Safety (drownings, accidents)
- Assault and harassment
- Environmental damage

### Media and Spring Break

**MTV Spring Break:**
- 1986-1999 (height)
- Live broadcasts from beaches
- Concerts, contests
- Spread the culture nationally

**Movies:**
- *Where the Boys Are* (1960, 1984 remake)
- Countless teen films
- Beach party movies
- Reinforced mythology

## Today

### The Changed Landscape

**Fort Lauderdale:**
- Upscale resort destination now
- Spring Break is controlled, minimal
- Different tourist target
- Rebranded successfully

**Panama City Beach:**
- Reduced dramatically
- Still some Spring Break
- More family-oriented push
- Learning from Fort Lauderdale

**South Beach:**
- Managed carefully
- High-end Spring Break
- Still draws crowds
- But controlled

### The Alternatives

**All-Inclusive Resorts:**
- Cancun, Jamaica packages
- Contained chaos
- Outside U.S. jurisdiction
- Still thriving

**Domestic:**
- Las Vegas
- Lake Havasu
- New Orleans
- Various beaches

### The Legacy

**What Florida Created:**
- An American institution
- A rite of passage
- A recurring problem
- A cultural phenomenon

- Expected ritual
- Industry serves it

**The Contradictions:**
- Freedom and danger
