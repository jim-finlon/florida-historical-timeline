# Vizcaya: James Deering's Italian Palace on Biscayne Bay
## 1916-Present

---

## Overview

Rising from the shores of Biscayne Bay like a Renaissance dream, Vizcaya is one of America's greatest architectural treasures and a monument to the Gilded Age's unbridled ambition. Built between 1914 and 1922 for International Harvester heir James Deering, the 34-room Italian Renaissance-style villa and its magnificent formal gardens required 1,000 workersâ€”ten percent of Miami's entire population at the time. Deering filled his winter palace with European antiques spanning 2,000 years, creating an instant heritage for a city that barely existed. Today, Vizcaya is Miami-Dade County's most visited museum and has hosted presidents, popes, and royalty, standing as proof that even in the Florida wilderness, anything could be built if you had enough money and vision.

## The Man

### James Deering

**The Background:**
- Born 1859, Maine
- Son of William Deering (farm equipment manufacturer)
- International Harvester Company heir
- Bachelor with refined tastes

**The Fortune:**
- Deering Harvester Company merged to form International Harvester (1902)
- One of America's largest corporations
- James became Vice President
- Enormous wealth

**The Personality:**
- Art collector
- World traveler
- Suffered from pernicious anemia
- Sought warm climate for health

### Why Miami

**The Choice:**
- Visited brother Charles in Coconut Grove
- Fell in love with Biscayne Bay
- Bought 180 acres (1910)
- Would build his masterpiece

## After Deering

### His Death

**1925:**
- James Deering died at sea
- Returning from Europe
- Age 65
- Never saw estate completed fully

**The Will:**
- Estate to nieces and nephews
- Maintained the property
- But expensive to keep
- Uncertain future

### The Hurricane

**1926:**
- Great Miami Hurricane
- Damaged Vizcaya
- Gardens flooded
- Recovery difficult

### The Decline

**The Depression:**
- Maintenance reduced
- Parts of estate sold
- Village buildings demolished
- Core preserved
