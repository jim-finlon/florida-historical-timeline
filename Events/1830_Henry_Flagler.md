# Henry Morrison Flagler: The Man Who Invented Florida
## 1830-1913

---

## Overview

No single person shaped Florida more than Henry Morrison Flagler. A co-founder of Standard Oil with John D. Rockefeller, Flagler became one of the richest men in America. In 1878, at age 48, he visited St. Augustine and saw potential where others saw a sleepy backwater. Over the next three decades, Flagler built the Florida East Coast Railway down the entire Atlantic coast, constructed grand hotels that made Florida fashionable, dredged harbors, built cities, and finally extended his railroad 128 miles over open ocean to Key West. When Flagler arrived, Florida was America's least populated state east of the Mississippi. When he died in 1913, Florida was on its way to becoming a destination for millions. Flagler didn't just develop Florida; he invented the modern tourist state.

## Early Life and Standard Oil

### The Rise

**Origins:**
- Born January 2, 1830, in Hopewell, New York
- Modest Presbyterian family
- Left school at 14 to work
- Moved to Ohio to make his fortune

**Early Career:**
- Worked in grain business
- Salt business during Civil War (failed)
- Left nearly bankrupt
- Started over

**Meeting Rockefeller:**
- Met John D. Rockefeller in Cleveland
- Recognized opportunity in oil
- Became partner in oil venture (1867)
- Standard Oil Company formed (1870)

### Standard Oil

**The Partnership:**
- Flagler: business strategy and logistics
- Rockefeller: operations and finances
- Together: unstoppable
- Built the largest company in America

**Flagler's Role:**
- Negotiated railroad rebates
- Designed corporate structure
- Legal and strategic mind
- Essential to Standard Oil's dominance

**The Wealth:**
- By 1880s, among richest men in America
- Standard Oil controlled 90% of U.S. oil refining
- Personal fortune in tens of millions
- Could afford any project

## Discovering Florida

### The First Visit (1878)

**The Trip:**
- Flagler visited Jacksonville and St. Augustine
- Wife Mary's health declining
- Seeking warm climate
- Found... possibility

**What He Saw:**
- Beautiful but undeveloped
- Potential for tourism
- No adequate hotels or transportation
- Opportunity

### The Second Visit (1881)

**After Mary's Death:**
- Mary Flagler died 1881
- Flagler married Alice Shourds 1883
- Returned to St. Augustine for honeymoon
- Vision crystallized

**The Decision:**
- Would build hotels in Florida
- Would build railroad to bring guests
- Would create a tourist industry
- Would transform the state

## Building the Empire

### St. Augustine (1885-1888)

- Grand Spanish Renaissance hotel
- Opened January 1888

- Thomas Edison installed electric lights

**The Alcazar Hotel:**

- Casino (entertainment, not gambling)

**The Impact:**
- St. Augustine became fashionable
- Wealthy Americans came
- Season established
- Florida was "discovered"

### The Railroad

**The Logic:**
- Hotels need guests
- Guests need transportation
- Railroad was key
- Flagler would build it

**The Strategy:**
- Bought existing short lines
- Extended, improved, connected
- Florida East Coast Railway
- His personal property

**The Extension:**

| Year | Reached |
|------|---------|
| 1889 | Daytona |
| 1894 | Palm Beach |
| 1896 | Miami |
| 1912 | Key West |

### Palm Beach (1893-1896)

**The Royal Poinciana Hotel:**
- Opened 1894
- Largest wooden structure in the world
- 1,150 rooms
- Playground for the rich

**The Breakers:**
- Originally Inn, then Hotel
- Oceanfront
- Still operating (rebuilt after fires)
- Iconic Palm Beach

**Palm Beach Itself:**
- Flagler created the resort
- Brought wealthy Americans
- Established the "season"
- Still exclusive today

### Miami (1896)

**Julia Tuttle's Invitation:**
- After the Great Freeze of 1894-95
- Tuttle sent orange blossoms to show Miami was warm
- Invited Flagler south
- He accepted

**The Extension:**
- Railroad reached Miami April 15, 1896
- Flagler built the city
- Dredged the harbor
- Created modern Miami

**The Royal Palm Hotel:**
- Miami's grand hotel
- Opened 1897
- Center of early Miami
- Flagler's vision realized

### Key West (1912)

**The Final Challenge:**
- 128 miles over open water
- "Flagler's Folly" critics called it
- Most ambitious construction project
- The "Eighth Wonder of the World"

**The Overseas Railroad:**
- Began 1905
- Completed January 22, 1912
- 82-year-old Flagler rode first train
- Greatest achievement

## Personal Life

### The Marriages

**Mary Harkness Flagler (1853-1881):**
- Married 1853
- Son Harry born
- Her illness brought Flagler to Florida
- Died 1881

**Alice Shourds Flagler (1883-1901):**
- Married 1883
- Became mentally ill
- Institutionalized 1897
- Marriage dissolved by special Florida law (1901)

**Mary Lily Kenan Flagler (1901-1913):**
- Married 1901
- Much younger
- Flagler's final years happy
- Inherited most of estate

### The Controversy

**The Divorce:**
- Alice Flagler in asylum
- Divorce impossible under normal law
- Florida legislature passed special law allowing divorce for insanity
- Critics saw it as Flagler buying a law
- "Flagler Divorce Law" later repealed

## Death and Legacy

### The Final Years

**After Key West:**
- Flagler's health declined
- Fell on marble steps at Whitehall (1913)
- Never recovered
- Died May 20, 1913, age 83

**The Burial:**
- St. Augustine
- Memorial Presbyterian Church (built for first wife)
- Tomb beside Mary and daughter

### What He Left

**Physical:**
- Florida East Coast Railway
- Hotels and resorts
- Cities: essentially created Miami, Palm Beach
- Infrastructure

**Economic:**
- Tourism industry
- Development pattern
- Agricultural shipping
- Florida's economy transformed

**Cultural:**
- Florida as destination
- The "season" concept
- Wealthy winter resort tradition
- Model for later development

## Assessment

### The Praise

**What He Built:**
- Transportation network
- Tourism industry
- Modern cities
- Economic foundation

**His Dedication:**
- Spent his fortune on Florida
- Worked into his 80s
- Vision and persistence
- Remarkable achievement

### The Criticism

**The Methods:**
- Robber baron tactics at Standard Oil
- Bought legislation (divorce law)
- Used wealth to shape government
- Ruthless businessman

**The Costs:**
- Workers died building railroad
- Environmental damage
- Displacement of some populations
- Gilded Age inequality

### The Balance

**The Man:**
- Complex figure
- Visionary and ruthless
- Builder and destroyer
- Transformative
