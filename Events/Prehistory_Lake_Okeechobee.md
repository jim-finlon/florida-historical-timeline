# Lake Okeechobee: Florida's Liquid Heart
## Prehistory-Present

---

## Overview

Lake Okeechobee—Florida's "inland sea"—is the largest freshwater lake in the American South, a 730-square-mile body of water that once served as the headwaters for the entire Everglades ecosystem. For millennia, seasonal overflows from Okeechobee fed the River of Grass; today, a massive dike and complex water management system have transformed the lake into a controlled reservoir, agricultural water source, and ecological battleground. The Seminoles called it "Okeechobee" (big water), and its fate—floods, pollution, algae blooms, and restoration efforts—reflects Florida's ongoing struggle to balance development with environmental survival.

## The Herbert Hoover Dike

### The Hurricanes

**1926 Miami Hurricane:**
- Moore Haven flooded
- Hundreds died
- Lake overflow deadly
- Calls for action

**1928 Okeechobee Hurricane:**
- Catastrophic disaster
- 2,500+ dead
- Lake surge devastated towns
- Worst natural disaster in Florida history

### Building the Dike

**The Response:**
- Army Corps of Engineers
- Herbert Hoover Dike constructed
- 143 miles around lake
- Flood control priority

**The Result:**
- Lake contained
- Natural flow blocked
- Everglades severed
- System changed forever
