# The Battle of Natural Bridge: Tallahassee's Salvation
## March 6, 1865

---

## Overview

The Battle of Natural Bridge, fought on March 6, 1865, was the last significant Confederate victory of the Civil War and saved Tallahassee from Union occupationâ€”making it the only Confederate state capital east of the Mississippi never captured by Union forces. The battle took place at a geological oddity where the St. Marks River disappears underground before reemerging half a mile downstream, creating a natural land bridge that became a defensive chokepoint. A hastily assembled force of Confederate troops, militia, and cadets from the West Florida Seminary (now Florida State University) repulsed a Union force attempting to march on Tallahassee. Today, Natural Bridge Battlefield Historic State Park commemorates this little-known but significant engagement.
