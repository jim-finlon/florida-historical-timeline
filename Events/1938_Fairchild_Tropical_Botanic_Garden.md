# Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden: A Living Encyclopedia of Tropical Plants
## 1938-Present

---

## Overview

Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden, established in 1938 on 83 acres in Coral Gables, is one of the world's great botanical gardens and the premier tropical plant collection in the continental United States. Named for renowned plant explorer David Fairchild, who introduced thousands of plants to America including mangoes, cherries, and nectarines, the garden showcases rare palms, cycads, flowering trees, and tropical species from every continent. Designed by landscape architect William Lyman Phillips, Fairchild combines scientific rigor with artistic beauty, featuring rainforest collections, vine pergolas, and stunning lake views against the backdrop of South Florida's unique environment. The garden remains a global leader in tropical plant research, conservation, and education.
