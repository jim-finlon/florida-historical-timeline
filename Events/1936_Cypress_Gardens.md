# Cypress Gardens: Florida's First Theme Park
## 1936-2009

---

## Overview

Before Walt Disney arrived in Florida, before SeaWorld or Busch Gardens, there was Cypress Gardensâ€”a botanical wonderland on the shores of Lake Eloise that opened in 1936 and pioneered the concept of the Florida theme park. Dick and Julie Pope created a Southern garden paradise with beautiful women in antebellum dresses, spectacular water-ski shows, and a brand of promotion so relentless that Cypress Gardens appeared in more than 50 films. For decades, Cypress Gardens was Florida's premier tourist attraction, the standard-bearer for the roadside attraction era, and the place where water skiing became a spectacle. Though the original park closed in 2009, its legacy lives on in the history of Florida tourism.
