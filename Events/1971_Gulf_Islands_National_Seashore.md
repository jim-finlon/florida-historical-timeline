# Gulf Islands National Seashore: America's Barrier Island Heritage
## 1971-Present

---

## Overview

Gulf Islands National Seashore, established in 1971, protects 160 miles of pristine barrier islands, beaches, and historic forts stretching from Mississippi to the Florida Panhandle. The Florida District, encompassing the Santa Rosa Island area from Fort Pickens to Perdido Key near Pensacola, preserves some of the most beautiful white-sand beaches in the country along with significant military fortifications dating from the early 19th century. The seashore offers visitors a unique combination of sun-drenched Gulf beaches, maritime forests, and historic structures that tell the story of coastal defense from Spanish colonization through World War II.
