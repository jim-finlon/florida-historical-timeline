# Marjorie Kinnan Rawlings and Cross Creek
## 1928-1953

---

## Overview

In 1928, a struggling writer named Marjorie Kinnan Rawlings bought a ramshackle orange grove in the remote Florida scrub near Cross Creek and found her voice. Over the next two decades, she would write the books that defined Florida's rural interior to the world, culminating in *The Yearling* (1938), which won the Pulitzer Prize and became an American classic. Rawlings immersed herself in the lives of the "Cracker" families around her, hunting, fishing, and farming alongside them while crafting prose of extraordinary lyrical beauty. Her home at Cross Creek is now a state park, and her books remain the definitive literary portraits of old Floridaâ€”a vanishing world she captured before it disappeared.

## The Yearling

### The Story

**The Plot:**
- Jody Baxter, young boy
- Parents eking out existence in scrub
- Jody adopts orphaned fawn, Flag
- Fawn must eventually be killed
- Loss of innocence

**The Characters:**
- Jody: dreaming boy
- Penny: wise, patient father
- Ora: embittered mother
- Flag: beloved fawn

### The Achievement

**The Recognition:**
- Published 1938
- Immediate bestseller
- Pulitzer Prize for Fiction (1939)
- Book-of-the-Month Club selection

**The Film:**
- MGM film 1946
- Gregory Peck starred
- Academy Award nominations
- Introduced story to millions

### The Significance

**The Legacy:**
- American classic
- Still taught in schools
- Defined Florida scrub for nation
- Universal themes of loss
