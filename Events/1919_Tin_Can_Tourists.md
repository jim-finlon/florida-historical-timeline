# The Tin Can Tourists: America's First RV Travelers
## 1919-Present

---

## Overview

In the winter of 1919, a group of automobile campers gathered in Tampa, Florida, and formed the Tin Can Tourists of the Worldâ€”one of the first organizations of recreational vehicle travelers in America. Named for the tin cans they used to cook meals, display as membership badges, and the tinned goods that sustained them on the road, these pioneers created a community of like-minded travelers who drove their cars and camping trailers to Florida each winter. At their peak in the 1930s, the Tin Can Tourists had over 100,000 members and established camps throughout Florida. They pioneered auto-tourism, invented the modern campground, and helped establish Florida as a destination for ordinary Americans, not just the wealthy.

## The Origins

### The Setting

**Post-World War I America:**
- Automobiles becoming affordable (Model T)
- Roads improving (slowly)
- Leisure time increasing
- Wanderlust growing

**Florida's Appeal:**
- Warm winters
- Escape from northern cold
- Affordable alternative to hotels
- Adventure

### The Pioneers

**The First Campers:**
- Drove Ford Model Ts and other cars
- Carried tents and supplies
- Cooked over campfires
- Improvised as they went

**The Challenge:**
- No established campgrounds
- Unreliable roads
- Car breakdowns common
- But freedom was worth it

### The Organization

**December 1919:**
- Group of campers gathered in Tampa
- Desoto Park campground
- Decided to organize
- Named themselves "Tin Can Tourists"

**The Name:**
- Tin cans for cooking, storing food
- Soldered tin can as membership badge
- Affectionate self-deprecation
- Contrast with wealthy tourists

## Significance

### For Tourism

**Democratizing Travel:**
- Before: Florida for the wealthy
- After: Florida for everyone
- Camping made travel affordable
- Middle class could winter in Florida

**Creating Infrastructure:**
- Campgrounds developed to serve them
- Became model for later RV parks
- Road improvements
- Service stations along routes

### For Florida

**Economic Impact:**
- Brought spending to Florida
- Stayed months, not days
- Spread beyond Miami and Palm Beach
- Developed inland Florida

**Cultural Impact:**
- Different kind of Florida visitor
- Working class and middle class
- Community builders
- Year-round population growth

### For RV Culture

**The Foundation:**
- Tin Can Tourists pioneered RV travel
- Created community model
- Developed trailer designs
- Established traditions

**The Legacy:**
- Modern RV clubs descended from this
- Snowbird phenomenon began here
- Campground industry roots
- American road trip tradition
