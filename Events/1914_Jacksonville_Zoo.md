# Jacksonville Zoo and Gardens: Florida's Historic Zoo
## 1914-Present

---

## Overview

The Jacksonville Zoo and Gardens, Florida's oldest and largest zoological institution, has welcomed visitors to Northeast Florida since 1914. Spanning 122 acres along the Trout River, the zoo houses more than 2,000 animals and features award-winning botanical gardens that have earned it recognition as one of the top zoo gardens in North America. From its humble beginnings with a single red deer fawn, the Jacksonville Zoo has evolved into a major conservation institution and the anchor of Jacksonville's cultural landscape.
