## Overview

NSU Art Museum Fort Lauderdale, housed in a dramatic building designed by Edward Larrabee Barnes, is one of the largest art museums in Florida and a leading institution for contemporary art in the southeastern United States. Founded in 1958 as the Fort Lauderdale Museum of the Arts, the museum has evolved into a cutting-edge contemporary art institution with particular strengths in Pop Art, Minimalism, and the CoBrA movement. Now affiliated with Nova Southeastern University, the museum occupies a 83,000-square-foot facility that anchors Fort Lauderdale's downtown arts district and serves as a vital cultural resource for South Florida.
