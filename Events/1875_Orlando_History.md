# Orlando: The City Beautiful
## 1875-Present

---

## Overview

Orlando, incorporated in 1875 as a small cattle town in Central Florida's lake-dotted interior, has transformed into one of America's fastest-growing metropolitan areas and the theme park capital of the world. Yet Orlando's story extends far beyond Disney and Universalâ€”from its origins as a frontier settlement and citrus hub, through its role in the space race and military aviation, to its emergence as a major convention destination, technology center, and diverse international city. "The City Beautiful" has evolved from a sleepy Southern town of 10,000 in 1940 to a booming metropolis of over 2.5 million in its metro area.

## History

### Early Settlement

**Origins:**
- Seminole Wars-era fort
- Cattle ranching country
- Named for soldier Orlando Reeves (legend)
- Or possibly Orlando Rees
- Settlement established 1840s

**Incorporation:**
- Town incorporated 1875
- Orange County seat
- Small frontier community
- Population under 1,000

### Citrus Era

**Orange Empire:**
- Citrus groves dominated landscape
- Orlando as shipping center
- Wealthy grove owners
- Beautiful Victorian homes

**The Freezes:**
- 1894-1895 Great Freeze
- Citrus industry devastated
- Industry moves south
- Orlando diversifies

## The Disney Revolution

### Before Disney

**1960s Orlando:**
- Population ~88,000
- Regional center
- Citrus and cattle
- Some tourism (Gatorland, etc.)

### Walt Disney's Secret

**The Purchase:**
- Walt Disney secretly buys land (1964-65)
- 27,000+ acres
- Shell companies hide identity
- Announcement shocks Florida (1965)

**The Impact:**
- Land prices skyrocket
- Speculation frenzy
- Infrastructure demands
- City transformed forever

### Walt Disney World Opens

**October 1, 1971:**
- Magic Kingdom opens
- 10,000 visitors first day
- Florida tourism transformed
- Orlando becomes destination

**Continued Growth:**
- EPCOT (1982)
- Disney-MGM Studios (1989)
- Animal Kingdom (1998)
- Resort expansion ongoing
