# George Merrick and the Creation of Coral Gables
## 1921-1926

---

## Overview

In the early 1920s, George Edgar Merrick transformed his family's citrus grove southwest of Miami into America's first fully planned community: Coral Gables. Unlike the chaotic speculation of the Florida land boom happening around him, Merrick envisioned a Mediterranean paradise with winding streets, plazas, ornamental gateways, and strict architectural standards. He hired artists and architects, created the legendary Biltmore Hotel, and marketed his creation with poetry and pageantry. When the 1926 hurricane and land bust destroyed his fortune, Merrick lost everything but left behind a city that remains one of America's most beautiful planned communitiesâ€”a living monument to one man's aesthetic vision.

## The Background

### The Merrick Family

**Solomon Merrick:**
- George's father
- Congregational minister from New England
- Moved family to South Florida 1899
- Purchased 160-acre homestead

**The Plantation:**
- Named it "Coral Gables" for coral rock and gabled house
- Grew citrus and vegetables
- George worked the grove
- Hard labor, big dreams

### George Merrick

**Born:**
- June 3, 1886
- Springdale, Pennsylvania
- Moved to Florida at age 12
- Raised on the grove

**Education:**
- Rollins College (briefly)
- New York Law School
- Studied art and literature
- Poet at heart

**The Character:**
- Dreamer and visionary
- Romantic temperament
- Business acumen
- Unusual combination

**The Inspiration:**

**The Concept:**

- Landscaping and beauty

## Building Coral Gables

### 1921: The Beginning

**The Launch:**
- Merrick began development
- First lots sold
- Construction began
- Vision taking shape

**The Marketing:**
- Hired William Jennings Bryan as spokesman ($100,000/year)
- Poetry and pageantry
- Venetian Pool created from quarry
- Bus tours for prospects

### The Components

**The Entrances:**
- Ornamental gateways
- Granada Entrance, Douglas Entrance
- Country Club Prado
- Dramatic arrivals

**The Streets:**
- Curved, not grid
- Named for Spanish places
- Landscaped medians
- Coral rock walls

**The Plazas:**
- DeSoto Plaza
- Ponce de Leon Plaza
- European-style gathering spaces
- Civic beauty

**The Buildings:**
- Congregational Church
- Country Club
- Commercial buildings
- All Mediterranean

### The Biltmore Hotel

**The Crown Jewel:**
- Opened January 1926
- 400 rooms
- 315-foot tower (replica of Giralda, Seville)
- Swimming pool: largest in continental U.S.

**The Grandeur:**
- Designed by Schultze and Weaver
- Cost: $10 million
- Hosted celebrities, royalty
- Symbol of the era

### The Venetian Pool

**The Creation:**
- Former coral rock quarry
- Transformed into swimming grotto
- Italian-style loggias
- Grottos and bridges
- Spring-fed, 820,000 gallons

**The Fame:**
- Most beautiful pool in America
- Still operating
- National Historic Register
- Merrick's genius

## The Boom and Bust

### 1925: The Peak

**The Numbers:**
- $150 million in sales
- Thousands of lots sold
- Development racing forward
- Merrick's empire

**The Expansion:**
- University of Miami founded (Merrick donated land)
- More hotels planned
- International village concept
- Grand ambitions

### 1926: The Collapse

**The Hurricane (September 1926):**
- Devastating storm hit Miami
- Killed hundreds
- Property destroyed
- Boom ended overnight

**The Bust:**
- Land values collapsed
- Sales stopped
- Mortgages defaulted
- Merrick's empire crumbled

### The Losses

**The Financial:**
- Merrick lost everything
- Personal fortune gone
- Company bankrupt
- Creditors everywhere

**The Personal:**
- Reputation damaged
- Some called him fraud
- Years of struggle
- Dreams destroyed

## After the Fall

### The Wandering Years

**The Exile:**
- Left Coral Gables
- Various jobs
- Fishing camp in Keys
- Postmaster in Islamorada
- Long years of obscurity

### The Return

**1940:**
- Appointed to Miami city commission
- Modest rehabilitation
- Recognized for contributions
- Partial vindication

### The Death

**March 26, 1942:**
- George Merrick died
- Age 55
- In Miami
- Relatively poor

## The Legacy

### What He Built

**Coral Gables:**
- Still Mediterranean in character
- Architectural standards maintained
- Beautiful and distinctive
- One of America's loveliest cities

**The Biltmore:**
- Still standing (renovated)
- National Historic Landmark
- Luxury hotel again

- National Historic Register
- Unique swimming experience
