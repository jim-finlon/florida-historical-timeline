# Miami Vice: The Show That Reinvented Miami
## 1984-1990

---

## Overview

On September 16, 1984, NBC debuted *Miami Vice*, a police drama that would transform not just television but the city it depicted. Created by Anthony Yerkovich and produced by Michael Mann, the show followed undercover detectives Sonny Crockett (Don Johnson) and Ricardo Tubbs (Philip Michael Thomas) through Miami's neon-lit drug underworld. With its pastel suits, designer stubble, rock-and-pop soundtrack, and stunning cinematography of Miami's Art Deco architecture, *Miami Vice* made the city the coolest place in America. The show didn't just reflect Miamiâ€”it reinvented it, sparking a renaissance of the Art Deco district, driving fashion trends, and permanently changing how Americans saw South Florida.

## The Setting

### Miami in 1984

**The Reality:**
- Cocaine cowboys era recent memory
- Mariel Boatlift (1980) still resonating
- High crime rates
- Reputation for danger

**The Irony:**
- City was troubled
- But visually stunning
- Art Deco gems decaying
- Potential waiting

**The Opportunity:**
- Television could reframe the narrative
- Show the beauty amid the danger
- Glamorize while depicting crime
- Create a new image

### The Pitch

**Anthony Yerkovich:**
- *Hill Street Blues* writer
- Idea: MTV cops
- Style as important as story
- Music driving narrative

**The Concept:**
> "MTV cops"

- Feature contemporary music
- Visual style paramount
- Miami as character
- Cool above all

## The Show

### The Premise

**The Characters:**

**Sonny Crockett (Don Johnson):**
- Miami-Dade Police detective
- Lives on a sailboat with pet alligator
- Drives a Ferrari (later Testarossa)
- Designer clothes, no socks
- Vietnam veteran

**Ricardo Tubbs (Philip Michael Thomas):**
- New York cop
- Came to Miami seeking brother's killer
- Stayed as Crockett's partner
- Sophisticated, stylish
- Perfect complement

**The Partnership:**
- Interracial buddy cop duo
- Undercover work
- Dangerous assignments
- Unorthodox methods

### The Style

**The Look:**

**Costume:**
- Pastel suits (pink, turquoise, white)
- T-shirts under sport coats
- No socks with loafers
- Designer stubble
- Ray-Ban sunglasses

**The Impact:**
- Set fashion trends nationally
- Men's fashion transformed
- "Miami Vice look" became phrase
- Influenced decade of style

**The Cinematography:**
- Shot on location in Miami
- Art Deco buildings prominently featured
- Neon lights
- Water, sky, pastels
- Gorgeous visuals

**The Music:**
- Contemporary rock and pop
- Phil Collins, Glenn Frey, Tina Turner
- Jan Hammer theme and score
- Music as narrative element
- MTV aesthetic

### The Themes

**The Content:**
- Drug trafficking
- Undercover dangers
- Moral ambiguity
- Personal costs of the job

**The Darkness:**
- Beneath the glamour
- Violence and death
- Corruption
- Human cost of drug trade

## Impact on Miami

### The Art Deco Renaissance

**Before** ***Miami Vice:***
- South Beach Art Deco district neglected
- Buildings deteriorating
- Plans to demolish for high-rises
- Barbara Baer Capitman fighting to save them

**After** ***Miami Vice:***
- National attention on architecture
- Tourists wanted to see the buildings
- Preservation movement energized
- Art Deco became iconic

**The Transformation:**
- South Beach revitalized
- Hotels renovated
- Restaurants opened
- Tourism exploded

### Tourism Boom

**The Numbers:**
- Tourism increased during show's run
- "Miami Vice tours" offered
- Visitors wanted to see filming locations
- International attention

**The Image:**
- From crime-ridden to glamorous
- Dangerous but exciting
- Stylish and cool
- Destination city

### Real Estate

**The Effect:**
- South Beach property values rose
- Investors took notice
- Development followed television
- Gentrification began

**The Irony:**
- Show about crime and drugs
- Made Miami desirable
- Violence as backdrop for glamour
- Complex messaging

## Cultural Impact

### Fashion

**The Influence:**
- Pastel colors for men
- Unstructured blazers
- Designer stubble acceptable
- No socks (briefly)
- Rolled-up sleeves

**The Reach:**
- International phenomenon
- Copied worldwide
- Defined 1980s style

### Television

**The Innovation:**

- Music integration
- Location shooting
- Cinematic quality

**The Legacy:**

- Influenced later shows

### Music

**The Soundtrack:**

- Episode songs became hits

## Reassessment

### What It Got Right

**The Vision:**
- Miami's visual potential
- Art Deco as treasure
- Multicultural city
- Stylish and dangerous

### What It Simplified

**The Reality:**
- Drug violence was devastating
- Marielitos unfairly stereotyped
- Poverty and inequality ignored
- Glamorized serious problems

### The Balance

**The Show Was:**
- Entertainment, not documentary
- Influential beyond intention
- Complex in impact
- Part of Miami's story now
